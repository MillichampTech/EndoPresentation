Mission statement

Endometriosis Awareness North Charity — Promote good health, prevent negative health outcomes, restore health & wellbeing.

What is endometriosis?

Endometriosis is a condition characterised by the presence of tissue resembling endometrium outside of the uterus.

Symptoms where endometriosis may be suspected

Chronic pelvic pain, dysmenorrhoea, deep pain during/after sex, gastrointestinal symptoms, urinary symptoms, infertility.

If pain persists during periods, is severe enough to interfere with normal activities, or lasts longer than 2–3 days, seek medical advice.

Key facts
UK impact
Estimated to affect 1.5 million women in the UK.
Global impact
Estimated to affect 190 million women globally.
Diagnosis delay
Average time to diagnosis: 7.5 years.
Average age at diagnosis
27 years.
Fertility
Difficulty conceiving can occur in 30–50% of cases.
Why care can be poor

Stigma around discussing period problems and painful sex, lack of awareness of symptoms, and gaps in specialist support and training.
